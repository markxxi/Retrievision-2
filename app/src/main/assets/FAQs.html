What is Retrievision?

Retrievision is a lost and found mobile application with automated image matching for STI College Calamba. The purpose of this application is to transfer most of the laborious lost and found process to the machine and leave only a few necessary actions to the users in order to promote efficiency and a streamlined process. Through such digitalization of lost and found, the tiresome work of manually searching around the campus, annoyance derived from the exhaustive writing of the descriptions of the lost items and their record keeping would be eliminated.

Retrievision then takes in these physical processes and related tools and assimilates them into one. Through the application, users can find a digital archive of the lost items they reported, be notified about the successful location of their lost belongings, and even be encouraged to participate in the volunteer work of finding and returning lost items due to an Integrity Board that promotes trust, competitiveness, and responsibility. By harnessing the competitive nature and the innate honesty of students and employees of the school, Retrievision aims to revolutionize the lost and found system for college life.

Why is there a need for Retrievision?

A persistent problem of human civilization is our ability to lose items quickly, made worse by our imperfect memory. Retracing our memories does not always yield great results, and most of the time we fail to remember key information. It is now up to others who find them or a lucky remembrance of key information that a person can find their lost item.

Naturally, this leads to frustrations and countless issues, and so we have come up with countless methods to fix this. The most common one used across the globe is the paper-based system. However, this traditional method suffers from certain problems, which also happened to plague STI College Calamba, problems that can be generalized into the following three:

  • Transparency and Communication Issues: There is a lack of readily available and accessible reminders and materials for instructions, and an efficient method to proceed with the lost and found process creates a needlessly complex process of physically asking random employees about what to do to enact the lost and found process.
  • Confusing and Tiring Return Process: Owners face time-consuming processes when reclaiming lost belongings. Having no immediate material to remember the instructions of the lost and found process, who to ask for help, where to put the items, and the overall wasted time and effort makes the return process not worth it for many people.
  • Management Problems: Intermediaries are burdened with multiple responsibilities and encounter complications due to traditional paper-based records. The Intermediaries are tasked with several responsibilities, including recording lost and found incidents, safekeeping lost items, and acting as mediators in the retrieval process. Finders and Owners heavily depend on intermediaries, especially if face-to-face communication is not possible.

4. What is a machine learning model?

Machine learning is a program that had been trained to locate and identify patterns, and enact its own decisions through feeding it specific datasets such as a dataset of images. To better understand this, let us use the example of a dog. A dog can be trained to do tricks by showing it certain command symbols and rewarding its correct behavior. Through trial and error, the dog slowly learns to do tricks because it then recognizes the pattern of doing the action imposed by your command symbol and being rewarded for doing it. Now, returning back to machine learning, the same is true for it, as it is rewarded with positive feedback for properly identifying the patterns you intended, and slowly it gets better and better like a professionally trained dog.

5. What is image recognition?

Image recognition is a tool for a device to identify the contents of an image through Computer Vision. If machine learning can be likened to the brain of a dog, then image recognition are the sensory organs of the dog. Paired with machine learning models, the image recognition tool would be used to scan and analyze the contents of an image to provide the description the user desires. Using the analogy of the dog again, the dog can be told to fetch a stick and it will use its sensory organ to find the object, analyze, and confirm its identity as the stick.

6. What are the contents of Retrievision and their use?

Sign In Page

Retrievision has four pages in total, locked behind a Sign-In page for security purposes. In order to access these pages, users must first do the following:

  1. Click the Sign In button.
  2. After the redirection to your browser to the Microsoft page, a permission request for Retrievision would appear. What you must do after such a request appears is to click the accept button to proceed or cancel to cease the process.
  3. Should you proceed, you would then be redirected to the home page, seen as a “House” icon at the bottom, within the row containing three other buttons such as a “Trophy” icon representing the Integrity Page, a square “Note” icon with a circle at its top right representing the Notifications Page, and a “Circle” icon that contains your profile picture representing your Dashboard.

Home Page

This contains the following:

  • Floor Box (Blue - Top): At the top of this page lies a blue box where within it lies the icons for the floors of STI, and clicking an icon would display below it the name of that floor and the current number of lost objects found there.
  • Match Box (Yellow – Center): At the center of this page lies a yellow box where within it lies the scan button. Clicking the button displays a popup box containing your reported objects, and at the right of each report is an icon for a “Camera.” Clicking this “Camera” icon would make the device scan the database for matches with your report, and should there be a match, the following would occur:
    1. A list of potential matches with your report would be displayed, and clicking one would present you with a choice to confirm the details.
    2. Should you proceed and confirm the object as yours, a notification would be sent to you and the Finder that there had been a successful match, and a claiming pass would be generated.
    3. Then follows your step to proceed to the Student Affairs Department to have the final verifications such as showing your claiming pass before you can retrieve your lost item.
  • Report Found Object Button (Light Yellow – Bottom, Above Report Lost Object Button): At the bottom of this page and above the Report Lost Object button lies this box for reporting found objects. Clicking it results in being redirected to a blue page where you would be asked to start the process. After clicking this, the procedure is as follows:
    1. You will be asked to open the camera to take a picture of the image.
    2. After the image has been taken, a description box would appear detailing what the application described it as. Should it not be the proper description, you can retake or just proceed to the next step to alter it manually. Should it be correct, then you can proceed.
    3. You would then be asked to fill up the remaining information, with some being optional and some being mandatory.
    4. After submission, you will be redirected to a new page to confirm the details of your report. What follows is either you go back to edit the details or submit and confirm.
    5. After submitting and confirming, you will be redirected to a page congratulating you for your successful report, with a button at the bottom to be able to return to the Home Page once again. Additionally, you will get a notification that pertains to the success of your found report.
    6. Then, you are to go to the Student Affairs Department to surrender the found item so that they can store it.
  • Report Lost Object Button (Blue – Bottom, Below Report Found Object Button): At the bottom of this page and below the Report Found Object button lies this box for reporting your lost objects. Clicking it results in being redirected to a blue page where you would be asked to start the process. After clicking this, the procedure is as follows:
    1. You will be asked to open your gallery to select the image of your lost object (If you have one) or skip and go directly to filling out the details manually.
    2. Should you choose the first option in the previous procedure, the application would analyze the image you provided and a possible description of the object would appear as a result. Should it not be the proper description, you can choose another image or try the current one or just proceed to the next step to alter it manually. Should it be correct, then you can proceed.
    3. You would then be asked to fill up the remaining information, with some being optional and some being mandatory.
    4. After submission, you will be redirected to a new page to confirm the details of your report. What follows is either you go back to edit the details or submit and confirm.
    5. After submitting and confirming, you will be redirected to a page congratulating you for your successful report, with a button at the bottom to be able to return to the Home Page once again. Additionally, you will get a notification that pertains to the success of your lost report.

Integrity Page

This exists to acknowledge students for their contribution in reporting subjects, and within this page lies the list that has the names of students and the number of their found items successfully returned to their owners as points. The list is arranged from highest points to lowest points, and to be able to be added to the list, the user must have at least one found item successfully returned to its owner.

Notifications Page

This page contains the list of the user’s notifications about their successfully reported lost or found items, successful matches, and successful return of their found items to their owners.

Dashboard Page

This page contains the following:

  • Information Box (Top): At the top of this page lies a box where within lies written your name, email, and also a circular icon that displays your profile at the left of the previous two.
  • Recent Surrendered Item Box (Right, Below Information Box): At the right of this page and below the information box lies this box that displays your recently surrendered lost item.
  • Found Item Box Button (Blue – Left, below Information Box): At the left of this page and below the information box lies the icon of a box that when clicked displays all of your reported found items and their details.
  • Lost Item Suitcase Button (Blue – Left, below Found Item Box Button): At the left of this page and below the icon for the found items lies the icon of a suitcase that when clicked displays all of your reported lost items and their details.
  • Personal Points Button (Yellow – Left, below Lost Item Suitcase Button): At the left of this page and below the icon for the lost items lies the icon of a trophy that when clicked shows your successfully surrendered found items as points.
  • Claiming Pass Ticket Button (Blue – Bottom Left, below Personal Points Trophy Button): At the left of this page and below the icon for your personal points lies the icon of a ticket that when clicked displays all of your successfully generated claiming pass.
  • Surrendered Items Hand Button (Blue – Bottom Center): At the bottom of this page and between the icon for the claiming pass and settings lies the icon of a hand with two small circles hovering slightly above it that when clicked would display all of your successfully surrendered found items.
  • Settings Cog Button (Yellow – Bottom, Right of Surrendered Items Hand Icon): At the bottom of this page and at the right of the icon for the surrendered items lies the icon of a cog that when clicked displays the setting page, which contains the following:
    1. Tutorial Button: This displays a detailed tutorial on how to use Retrievisions’ features.
    2. FAQs Button: This displays these FAQ's.
    3. Privacy Policy Button: This displays the details of the privacy policy of the application.
    4. Terms and Conditions Button: This displays the details of the terms and conditions users are made to agree upon and consent to upon download or utilization of the application and its features.
    5. About Us: This displays the details of the developers of Retrievision and those related to its development.
    6. Sign Out Button: This undoes your sign in session and returns you to the Sign In page. If you want to access the locked contents of the application, then you must redo the Sign In procedure.

7. How would I know if my lost item has been found?

You would receive a notification every time a user reports a lost item. But that is a general notification, and to precisely know if your lost item has been found, then you must proceed with using the scanning feature located at the yellow box at the center of the home page. Once, you have followed the procedures, you would be shown a list of potential matches which would allow you to check them for their details and know if they are your lost item or not.

8. How long does a found and lost item report last?

These reports last up to three months and would be cleared from user view and sent to archive after such length has passed. However, the length is not permanent and is subject to change.

9. Where are the found items being stored?

Found items are stored in the Students Affairs Department for safekeeping.

10. How long does a found item stay in the Student Affairs Department?

Just like the duration length of reports, found items are kept in the Student Affairs Department for three months. Again, the length is not permanent and is subject to change.

11. What happens to found items after their storage length in the Student Affairs Department has passed?

Found items that have not been claimed by anyone would be donated to charity every three months.
